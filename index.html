Ujjayanta Palace

The Royal Crown of Agartala, Tripura

Named by Nobel Laureate Rabindranath Tagore

Plan Your Visit

Royal Legacy

Standing majestically in the heart of Agartala, Ujjayanta Palace is a magnificent 19th-century royal residence that served as the seat of the Manikya Dynasty, which ruled Tripura for over 1,000 years [^3^]. Built by Maharaja Radha Kishore Manikya between 1899 and 1901, this gleaming white edifice covers approximately one square kilometer in the capital city [^4^].

The palace derives its poetic name from Nobel Laureate Rabindranath Tagore, who was so captivated by its beauty that he not only named it "Ujjayanta" but also penned his renowned poem "The Palace" inspired by its grandeur [^1^]. After Tripura became a state of India, the Government purchased the palace, which later housed the State Legislative Assembly until 2011 [^3^].

🏛️ Did You Know?

The palace compound stretches across approximately 250 acres with expansive Mughal-style gardens, pools, and fountains. The surrounding grounds cover nearly 800 acres, making it one of the most spacious royal complexes in Northeast India [^1^].

Architectural Marvel

Designed by British architects, the palace showcases a unique fusion of Indo-Saracenic and Neoclassical architectural styles, reminiscent of Agra's Taj Mahal [^2^].

🏰

Three Majestic Domes

The palace features three soaring domes, with the central dome reaching an impressive 86 feet high, creating a stunning skyline against the Agartala sky [^1^].

🎨

Chinese Room

Adorned with exquisite tiles and a ceiling crafted by artisans brought from China, showcasing international craftsmanship and royal opulence [^1^].

💎

Belgian Glass Ceiling

The palace boasts an incredible Belgian glass ceiling that filters natural light into the royal chambers, creating an ethereal atmosphere [^1^].

🐘

Royal Throne

A magnificent throne made from elephant tusks stands as a testament to the craftsmanship and regal splendor of the Manikya dynasty [^1^].

🌸

Mughal Gardens

Huge Mughal-style gardens beautified by serene pools, vibrant flora, and musical fountains create a picturesque oasis surrounding the palace [^3^].

🚗

Royal Carriage

Designed by the renowned British firm Martin & Burn, the royal carriage was used to transport both the King and Queen in ceremonial processions [^1^].

State Museum

Today, Ujjayanta Palace houses the State Museum of Tripura, offering visitors an engaging journey through the region's rich cultural tapestry with 22 display galleries [^6^].

🏺 Royal Artifacts

Explore the legacy of the Manikya Dynasty through an impressive collection of royal artifacts, coins, and memorabilia that adorned the palace halls for centuries.

⚔️ Weapons Gallery

View an extensive collection of historical weapons, arms, and armory used by the royal forces and tribal warriors of ancient Tripura.

🧵 Textiles & Handlooms

Discover the rich weaving traditions of Tripura, featuring exquisite handloom textiles, traditional costumes, and intricate embroidery work.

🎨 Paintings & Art

Admire rare paintings and artworks that depict the cultural life, royal ceremonies, and natural beauty of the Tripura kingdom.

🎭 Tribal Heritage

Learn about the indigenous communities of Tripura through displays of traditional jewelry, bamboo crafts, and tribal cultural artifacts.

📜 Manuscripts

View ancient manuscripts and historical documents that chronicle the 1,000-year reign of the Manikya Dynasty over the region.

How to Reach

✈️ By Air

Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport (IXA) is located 12 km northwest of Agartala [^4^]. It is the second busiest airport in Northeast India after Guwahati, with daily flights by Air India and other major carriers connecting to major Indian cities.

🚂 By Train

Agartala Railway Station is located 5.5 km from the city center [^4^]. It is the first state capital of independent India connected to the national rail network, with direct connections to Guwahati and the rest of India via broad gauge.

🚌 By Road

Agartala is well-connected via National Highway 44 [^4^]. Key distances: Guwahati (785 km), Shillong (686 km), Silchar (460 km), Aizawl (293 km), and Dharmanagar (173 km). Regular bus and cab services are available.
